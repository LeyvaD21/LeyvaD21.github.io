Design Project 3

Goals: The overall project goal is to learn to create a 3D Printed, and laser cut enclosure for a light sounds soldering kit. By First learning to solder and compose a kit, we then begin to measure and design the enclosures a 1st iteration of 3-D print, and then a 2nd enclosure for laser cutting. The idea for the enclosure is to have access to 3 features, to be able to see the lights, allow the microphone to hear, and have access for the batteries.

Summarizing the Project

For CAD, I think that it would be alot better to change the design aspect to a smaller point. In terms of variables, I really enjoyed how simple it is to manage and create the shapes, holes, sizing, etc., but there were much more customizable options for the individual sides of the project, and work better with angular angles. At first the customization and training was super easy, but once I tried getting a bit more creative with different shapes and positions, the software became a tad bit complicated, especially without a mouse. I think little details like that can make a huge difference, but as they say, simple is also complicated.

Fusion360 was a lot harder to use, in terms of the complexity of services that it offers, but once I got the hang of it, the workflow began to make sense, and the blend of using the solid and mesh tabs began to work in my favor. Using software like this is really cool, because it's so complicated that once it starts to make sense, my world becomes endless and it's awesome. Struggling with creating a platform for the hollow shark, and adjusting for the chicken took me a while, but once I was able to work the blend of the bodies, it all went uphill from there.

All in all, this project was a lot more fun but also just as intense, if not more when it came to using the software. It was fun to play around TinkerCad and really try hard on Fusion360. Personally, I think I would use Fusion a lot more because it has so many more capabilities, and the possibilities really are endless with how many things it provides. I think once I learn how each specific setting interacts with one another, blending and meshing, and hopefully animation at one point, it’s going to become extremely helpful.

Drawing:HotDog Train

For the drawing aspect using TinkerCad, I wanted to create something funny and really something I've been craving. A hot dog on a cart. But how about a Hot Dog on a TRAIN cart? Initially, I was looking for actual hot dogs, but because of file issues, I settled in for a hot dog(weiner dog) and decided that that would be just a good mixture of the two. When looking around thingiverse, I looked for the best component of the mixture, which began with taking off the body of the train, and mixing in a weiner dog body that was in the format of the hot dog bun. However, the body of the dog was not shaped like a hot dog bun, but that was okay.

TinkerCAD

Once I transferred it into CAD, this process was a lot easier to use than fusion360, and not as complicated as Prusa itself. When it came to editing the design, the tutorials really helped a lot, and most specifically in the editing of simple designs. I don't think TinkerCAD can create a lot more complex designs, given the shapes, tools that it offers, which is great for simple designs like this. Through my process, the editing was easy in subtracting the train carts, and adding the body to the train. Besides that, there was no other struggle when it came out using TinkerCAD, and I can definitely see myself using it alot more.

Prusa Slicing the Dog

Once I transferred the design into PrusaSlicer, the first suggestion it gave me was to reshape the size, which I did because it was tiny. Besides that, there were no other suggestions for it, but I decided to add support to it because I was afraid that the tip of the nose of the dog would result in a failure. So as a failsafe I ended up adding supports everywhere on the project, and wanted to decide on the cleaning up later.

Cleaning and Finishing

As you can see, the product did come out really full of support, which in my head ended up being a good thing, because it meant that there are little defects to it. Upon a closer inspection, we can see that there are a lot of issues when it comes to the curvature of the train body, and specifically the support overhand. Underneath it, there is a lot of splitting and under-extrusion, leaving many open gaps around the perimeters and infills of the curves. While there are also some gaps alongside the head, the rest of the print looks like a success. It's still hard to determine whether or not the print will have some failures or not, but it's a learning curve. Overall, I'm pretty happy with the print itself, it was very easy to use TinkerCAD as a first software design, and the print looks well done. The layers look great, and once we sliced and looked at the process, it was easy sailing through that. Withbeing told to use two designs to subtract/add to it using TinkerCAD, helped me to understand much more about how the printing process works, and the little tricks that we need to overlook for a successful print. While there are many options to choose from, using TinkerCAD was a good first run to edit designs themselves, but I will say that although it's very simple, there are a lot of huge limitations to the creativity we can do. I enjoy seeing the elements

Drawing PolloShark

For my drawing, I was trying to play around Fusion360, as a CAM software that is a lot tougher to use. For my drawing, I had in mind to use a shark head that used more triangular shapes to play around the tools of much more specifics and details that can be used. Additionally, With such a complex design, i believed why not to include a very simple design, which can be built as blocks, which resulted in a minecraft chicken. These two blends felt like a great way to play around with a complex and simple design, seeing how the merging of both of them can work.

Fushion360

This software was A LOT harder to use and manage because of all the details that it includes, both with CAD and CAM software. At first, the tutorials were very helpful but it did get repetitive, which is when I began to play around more with what was going on. At some point I decided to export the shark head and the minecraft chicken onto the platform. At first, it was very easy to move and adjust the objects to where I wanted. However, I ended up struggling with creating a platform with the mesh body and a solid body, which took me a while to fix. And I did so by creating a cylinder object and transitioning it to become a mesh body, which then allowed me to mesh both objects and that platform itself. I struggled the most with Fusion360, but once I figured it out, the design aspect of Solid and Mesh tabs became easier to use.

PolloShark PrusaSlicer

Once I was able to export it to Prusa Slicer, the size was Huge compared to the capabilities of the machine, so i ended up reducing it to a 2 scale size which reduced it immensely, but thanks to how compact the design is, there was no need to use any type of support, initially. At first the print was going great, until midway on the chicken, where the failure happened upon the small layer of feet, that the temperature and speed that it was being built made it overheat, so it didn't stick at all and made it ooze. I ended up creating a second print, with natural supports that is focused around the chicken,which will greatly improve the structure itself. It's beginning to become easier to use PrusaSlicer, now that I’m learning to change the print settings themselves, in terms of speed and such, to work the way that I want it to work.

Updated and Finished Shark

For my first shark pollo print, it ended up looking really well done when it came to the shark and the platform individually, but the chicken was a total failure. This was due to having such a small area of print that had high temperature and speed, that resulted in it stringing and not cooling fast enough to stay upright. Thus for the second print, I added natural support to prevent this from happening inside the mouth of the shark. My second shark print, which is shown here, was a total success. Everything looked great and the natural supports I changed instead of full supports was alot better than how I expected. The print looked great, there are little to none mistakes. In the picture above I havent cleaned it entirely, but overall im happy with a very successful print, and with increasing it in size as well, the print is sturdy and colorful.
