Pulley Clip Iteration

In the next couple of iterations that we worked on, we had our CAD Design Specialist, Domenic, use his amazing and wonderful thinking cap to produce an amazing design. By creatring a secure and stable design, he came up with a pulley clip that did not hinder the movment of the tube, and offered uspport to the sides of the tubing. This design was created on TinkerCAD, and had two iterations of one or two pulleys.

His desing was a success when done printing, and offered amazing insight to how we can contiune to customize and design other options for the tubing.

This iteration of the pulley system had two options, a single and a double pulley. Bt having a smaller clip size for the drainage tubing, we determined it was fir best use in lateral movement, and used magents to attach itself to any secure location. Small pins were used for the axled wheels in order to be easy to remove and attach regarldess of where it is used.

The second iteration was used by doing two pulleys instead of one on the opposite side of the clip. This addition was added in order to increase area of contact for the tubing, for longer distances to continue to provide adequate movement alongside the patient.

Why are we working on this?

The goal for this porkect was to work on a product that is both unique, and often overlooked wehn it comes to medical devices. 3-D prints are going to become the pinnacle of technology in the near future, and its important to look at not just the variables, but possibilties of the things we can manufacture with our current situation.

At first we started with a client, but they decided that they were no longer interested. We are still working on the same general problem but without a specific intended client with the intent of releasing our solution as an open-source hardware with a guide on how our clips owuld wokr for a night ostomy bag. We're excited to see how far laong we can contiune to design anf test our product before we upload it for someone to use!

Despite not having a client, we contined to work on remixing some of the previous solutions we came up with, the clip and velcro in order to mitigate the risks of damaging or clogging the tube. As we kept operating with different designs, my team desided that to be effective, we need larger diamteres rather than perfect fit, in order to provide the best functionality for ostomy patients.
