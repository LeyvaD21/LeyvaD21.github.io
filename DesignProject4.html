Slice and Build

This project began once we were able to open the STL files to download the Unlimited Phoenix hand V3 hand, to which I downloaded the left hand. For this project, we were using IdeaMaker slicer, and imported the parts and also scale them to 150%. Once we did that, I used the Raised3D printer, which has two extruders, and was able to have all of the components in the print, besides the Phalanx. After loading the Raise3D with new filament, the print was on its way!

Using the IdeaMaker was new to me, but it was extremely easy to use, and showed a succesful slice when I imported all of the pieces I wanted. The print took more than a day.

Hand Assembly

My partner and I were able to have all of our compontents printed out by the nxt class, and we accounted for prioritizing the joints and pins. By watching a tutotial video on assembly, and using the e-Nable instructions worksheet, we were able to fully complete the hand. The process itself was really fun and easy, most of it just looking for the right size pin, and fiting it in for fingers. Extremly straightforward and easy, the only difficult part was the stringing of the hands. We had a harder time trying to find the right tension for the hand, but in the end we fine tuned that and got a great outcome on the hand closing.

In the End we ended up completing the hand and it functioned extremly well. Unfortunately I have fat hands so I cannot put my hand all the way in, but im happy that our first hand build was a success!

Remixing the Hand

The remix that I had in mind was to make it easier to brush our pets. The inspiration came for my cat back home, who sheds too much and somehow there is never a brush in sight. I looked into Thingsverse and found this brush that initially had a handle on it, cut it to only have the part I wanted, and fitted it onto the palm of the hand to what I wanted it to be. For this, It was quite simple to do. The idea was to take the brush, initially with a handle, to cut that part off and elongage the edges to then combine it with the palm of the hand using Fusion360.I scaled the brush to better fit the hand, and from that point made minor adjsutments to send it over to Prusa to slice!
